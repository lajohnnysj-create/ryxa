Impersonation Policy

Effective date: June 1, 2026

Ryxa pages should be a genuine reflection of the person, brand, or organization behind them. Pretending to be someone else is not allowed, and this rule applies to every page on Ryxa, whether or not the page is verified.

This policy works alongside our Community Standards and Terms of Service. Where they overlap, the Terms of Service take precedence.

What impersonation is

Impersonation is using a Ryxa page to pretend to be another person, brand, or organization in a way that could mislead visitors. It is about deception, presenting yourself as someone you are not, or as officially representing someone you do not.

This can include using another person's name, photos, likeness, logo, or branding to make people believe a page is theirs or is endorsed by them, when it is not.

What is allowed

Allowed

  • Fan pages, parody, commentary, and tribute pages, as long as they are clearly labeled as such and would not lead a reasonable visitor to think they are the real person or an official page.
  • Managing an official page on behalf of a person, brand, or organization when you are authorized to do so, for example as part of their team or agency.
  • Using a stage name, brand name, or handle that you genuinely operate under.

The test is simple. If a visitor can clearly tell who is really behind the page, and is not misled into thinking it is someone else, it is not impersonation.

What is not allowed

Not allowed

  • Claiming to be a specific person when you are not them.
  • Creating a page that copies a real creator's name, photos, or branding to pose as their official page.
  • Recreating someone's page to divert their audience, collect their payments, or take advantage of their reputation.
  • Presenting a page as the official presence of a brand or organization without authorization.
  • Using a parody or fan label as a cover while still trying to deceive people into thinking the page is genuine.

How this relates to verification

The blue check helps audiences identify a creator's authentic page, but impersonation is enforced on every page regardless of verification status. You do not need to be verified to be protected by this policy, and you do not need to be verified to report a page pretending to be you.

If you want to learn how the blue check works or how to get verified, see Verification and the Blue Check.

How to report impersonation

If a Ryxa page is pretending to be you, your brand, or an organization you represent, email us and we will review it. To help us act quickly, include:

  • The link to the Ryxa page you are reporting.
  • Who is being impersonated, and your relationship to them.
  • Something that shows you are the real person or are authorized to represent them. A link to your established, official social account is usually the clearest proof.

We may ask for additional information to confirm your identity or authority before taking action. This protects everyone involved and helps us avoid acting on a false report.

What happens next

When we confirm that a page breaks this policy, we may remove the page or specific content, limit features, or suspend or terminate the account. Clear or repeated impersonation can result in removal without further warning. If a verified page is found to be impersonating, the blue check is removed as part of that action.

This policy may change as Ryxa grows. When we make meaningful changes, we will update the effective date at the top of this page.
