Why Does My Social Media Connection Disconnect

Occasional disconnects are a normal part of how social platforms work, and in most cases they are not a sign that anything is broken. Connections can drop for reasons that come from the platform itself, and Ryxa is built to reconnect quietly in the background whenever it can. When a connection genuinely cannot be restored on its own, we let you know in your dashboard so you are never left guessing.

This article explains what a connection actually is, how Ryxa keeps it alive, and the common reasons one gets lost.

What a connection actually is

When you connect an account such as Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Twitch, or Facebook, you are not handing Ryxa your password. Instead, the platform gives Ryxa a secure access token: a temporary key that grants permission to read your public stats, like your follower count and engagement. That token is what powers your Media Kit numbers, your Link in Bio follower totals, and your analytics.

These tokens are designed to be temporary by nature. They expire on a schedule and have to be refreshed regularly to keep working. This is a security feature built into every major platform, not a flaw, and it is the reason a connection needs occasional upkeep rather than lasting forever untouched.

How Ryxa keeps your accounts connected

Ryxa handles token renewal for you automatically in the background. On a regular cycle, we refresh each connected account's token so your stats stay current without you doing anything. The vast majority of the time this happens silently and you never notice.

Every so often a single refresh does not go through, often because the platform was briefly unavailable at that exact moment. When that happens, Ryxa does not immediately give up and mark your account as disconnected. Instead we treat it as a temporary hiccup and try again on the next cycle. We only flag a connection as needing your attention when the platform itself tells us the token can no longer be renewed. In other words, we ask you to reconnect when it is genuinely necessary, not at the first sign of a passing glitch.

You will always be told. If a connection truly needs to be re-established, a clear notification appears in your dashboard naming the account, and its status is shown in Settings. You do not have to keep checking on your own.

Common reasons a connection is lost

When a connection does need to be re-established, the cause is usually on the platform's side rather than Ryxa's. Here are the most common reasons a platform ends a connection:

  • You changed your password. Most platforms automatically invalidate existing app connections when you change your account password, as a security precaution.
  • You logged out everywhere, or signed in on a new device or browser. Signing in fresh elsewhere, or ending all active sessions, can reset the connection tied to your previous session.
  • You revoked Ryxa's access. If you removed Ryxa from the connected-apps or security settings on the platform, that intentionally ends the connection until you reconnect.
  • The platform revoked the token for security. Platforms will sometimes cancel a token on their own if they detect unusual activity or a possible security concern on your account.
  • The token reached the end of its life. Some tokens have a maximum lifespan, and if too much time passes the platform may decline to renew it, requiring a fresh connection.
  • A temporary platform outage. If the platform was down at the moment a refresh was attempted, that single attempt fails. These usually recover on the next cycle without any action from you.

How to reconnect

Reconnecting is quick and fully restores your stats. Open your dashboard, go to Settings, find the account that needs attention, and reconnect it. The platform will confirm your permission, and Ryxa picks the connection back up from there.

If an account keeps disconnecting shortly after you reconnect it, it is worth checking a couple of things on the platform's side: make sure you have not recently changed your password, and check that Ryxa's access has not been removed from the platform's connected-apps settings. Those two will keep ending the connection until they are settled.
