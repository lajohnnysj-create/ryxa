My Account Got Banned

If you have found this page, your Ryxa account may have been suspended or banned. We know that is stressful, especially if your page is part of how you earn. This article explains what a suspension usually means and how to appeal it.

Why accounts get banned

An account is almost always suspended for one reason: something on the account was found to violate our Community Standards or our Terms of Service. These are the rules every Ryxa account agrees to follow.

Common reasons include linking to content that is not allowed on the platform, using Ryxa to advertise illegal or regulated goods, harassment, impersonation, spam, or other activity covered in the Community Standards. In most cases, the account holder has a sense of which rule was involved.

When an account is suspended, its public pages, including the Link in Bio page and any other public Ryxa pages, stop being visible to visitors. Access to the dashboard is also limited.

If you think it was a mistake

Suspensions are not always final, and we do review them. If you believe your account was suspended in error, or if you have corrected the issue and want your account reviewed again, you can appeal.

To appeal, email us at hello@ryxa.io. Include the email address or username on your Ryxa account, and a short explanation of why you believe the suspension should be reviewed. If you know which content was involved, mentioning that helps us look into it faster.

We review appeals individually. We cannot promise a specific outcome or timeline, but every appeal sent to that address is read by a person and looked into.

What to expect

After you send an appeal, we will review the account and the reason it was suspended. Some suspensions are reversed, for example when a violation was a misunderstanding or has since been fixed. Others are upheld, particularly for serious violations of the Community Standards.

Reading the Community Standards before you appeal is worth the few minutes. It will help you understand what may have triggered the suspension and write a clearer appeal.

Submit an appeal

If you believe your account was suspended in error, or you have resolved the issue, email us with your account details and we will review it.
