Verification and the Blue Check

Last updated: June 1, 2026

The blue check on a Ryxa page shows that the page is run by the real creator behind a known social presence. It is about authenticity, confirming you are who you say you are, not about fame, follower count, or importance.

This guide explains what the check means, who can apply, the two ways to verify, and what to expect after you submit.

What the blue check means

A verified Ryxa page is one we have confirmed belongs to the genuine creator. When a visitor sees the check next to your name, it tells them this is your real page and not someone pretending to be you.

Hovering over the check on a public page shows the message, "This profile is verified as belonging to the creator." That is the whole promise behind it: authenticity of ownership.

Who can apply

Verification is available to creators on an active Pro or Max plan. If you are on the Free plan, you will be prompted to upgrade before you can submit a request. You can compare plans on the pricing page.

A paid plan lets you apply. It does not grant the badge automatically. Every request is reviewed by a person before a badge is added.

The two ways to verify

You can prove the page is yours in one of two ways. Both confirm the same thing, that you control the social presence the page claims to represent.

Connected account

Best method

Connect your Instagram account to Ryxa under Settings, then Connected Accounts. When you apply this way, we confirm your identity using that secure connection, so there is nothing to type and nothing to wait on for proof. This is the fastest and strongest method, because the link to your account is confirmed directly rather than claimed.

Public profile link

Tougher process

If you would rather not connect an account, you can add a link to your Ryxa page in your public social bio, then give us the URL of that profile. We check that the profile genuinely links back to your Ryxa page. This route is reviewed more strictly and can take longer, since the proof is manual.

How to apply

  1. Open your dashboard and go to Link in Bio.
  2. Find the Verification section in the editor settings.
  3. Select Submit for verification. A short form opens.
  4. Choose your method, enter your first and last name, and confirm the agreement.
  5. Submit. Your status changes to pending while we review.

If you chose the connected account method but no Instagram account is connected yet, connect it first under Settings, then Connected Accounts, and come back to apply.

Review and timing

Requests are reviewed by a person, typically within 3 to 5 business days. While your request is open, the Verification section shows a pending state. If your request is approved, the blue check appears on your public Link in Bio page. If we cannot approve it, you are free to submit a new request once any issue is resolved.

When a profile needs extra review

Most requests are straightforward. Some need a closer look before we can approve them. Common reasons include:

  • The social account you are verifying with is brand new.
  • The account is not yet well established, with little history, content, or activity.
  • There is more than one page or account using the same name, so it is not yet clear which one is authentic.
  • The name on your Ryxa page does not clearly match the account you are verifying with.
  • The account recently changed its handle or display name.
  • Someone else has already claimed or applied to verify the same identity.
  • The connection or link-back could not be confirmed cleanly during review.

In these situations, we will reach out by email to ask for a little more information before we make a decision. This is a normal part of review and does not mean your request will be declined.

We do not ask for documents. Even when we follow up for more information, we will never ask you for a passport, government ID, or any paperwork. We are only confirming that you control the account your page represents.

Keeping your badge

The blue check stays as long as your page remains eligible and in good standing. A few things to know:

  • It requires an active Pro or Max plan. If your subscription ends and your account returns to Free, the badge is removed.
  • It can be removed if a page is found to break our policies, including impersonation.
  • If your badge is removed, your Verification section resets so you can apply again once you are eligible.

What verification is not

To set expectations clearly:

  • It is not a measure of fame, status, or follower count. A large following does not earn it, and a small one does not prevent it.
  • It is not a legal identity check, and we do not ask for documents. We confirm that you control the creator presence the page represents, not a passport or government ID.
  • It is not an endorsement of your content. It only speaks to who runs the page.

Impersonation

The blue check helps your audience tell your real page apart from fakes. Impersonation itself is handled separately and applies to every page on Ryxa, whether or not it is verified. If someone is pretending to be you, you do not need to be verified to report it. See our Impersonation Policy for how that works and how to report a page.

Ready to get verified?

Open your dashboard, head to Link in Bio, and look for the Verification section to submit your request. You will need an active Pro or Max plan.
