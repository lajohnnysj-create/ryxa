How to Block Time Slots in 1:1 Bookings

Your 1:1 booking availability is built from two things: the weekly hours you set on the booking service, and your Ryxa Calendar. Any event on your calendar automatically removes that time from the slots clients can book, so you never get double-booked over something you already have scheduled.

That means the simplest way to block a time slot is to add an event to your calendar for it. Clients see fewer available slots, but they never see what the event is.

Your clients only see open and unavailable. When you block time with a calendar event, bookers just see that slot as taken. The event title, notes, and details are never shown to them.

Block a one-time slot

Use this when you want to make yourself unavailable for a specific date and time, for example, an afternoon off next Tuesday.

  1. Open your Ryxa dashboard and go to Calendar.
  2. Click Add Event.
  3. Give it a title (only you will see it, so name it whatever helps you, like Lunch or Blocked).
  4. Set the date, start time, and end time for the period you want to block.
  5. Click Save Event.

That time will immediately drop out of your 1:1 booking availability for that date.

Block a recurring slot

Use this for times you are regularly unavailable, a daily lunch break, a standing meeting every Tuesday, or a weekly commitment. Instead of adding the same block over and over, you create it once as a repeating event.

  1. In Calendar, click Add Event.
  2. Set the title, date, start time, and end time for the first occurrence.
  3. Under Repeat, choose how often it repeats: Daily, Weekly, Monthly, or Yearly.
  4. Under Ends, choose when the repeat should stop:
    • Never, the block repeats indefinitely.
    • On date, the block repeats until a date you pick.
    • After # of times, the block repeats a set number of times.
  5. Click Save Event.

Every occurrence of that repeating event blocks its time slot automatically, so a "Lunch, every weekday, 12:00 to 1:00 PM" event keeps that hour off your booking calendar going forward without any extra work.

Change or remove one occurrence of a recurring block

Sometimes you want to keep a recurring block but adjust a single day, for example, your usual Tuesday block moves an hour this week, or you want to reopen one specific lunch. When you edit or delete a repeating event, Ryxa asks how far the change should reach:

  • This event, changes or removes only the occurrence you picked. The rest of the series stays the same.
  • This and following events, changes or removes that occurrence and every one after it, leaving earlier ones untouched.
  • All events, applies to the whole repeating series.

To reopen a single blocked slot, open that occurrence, choose Delete, and pick This event. That one slot becomes bookable again while the rest of your recurring block stays in place.

A note on time zones

Your calendar and your booking availability both run on the calendar time zone set in Settings › Calendar. Clients always book in their own local time zone, and Ryxa converts between the two automatically, so a block you set for 12:00 PM your time correctly removes the matching moment for a client in another time zone. If your blocks ever look an hour off, check that your calendar time zone in Settings matches where you actually are.
