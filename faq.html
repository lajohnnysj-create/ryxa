General
What is FindTheSnakes? ▼

FindTheSnakes is a tool that lets you upload your Instagram data export files and instantly see who you follow that isn't following you back. We call them snakes 🐍

Unlike other tools, we never ask for your Instagram password or connect to your account. Everything is based on files you export directly from Instagram yourself.

How does FindTheSnakes work? ▼

It's simple — Instagram lets you download your own data as JSON files. You download those files, upload them here, and we instantly compare your followers list against your following list to show you who isn't following back.

Your files are processed entirely in your browser and are never sent to our servers.

Is FindTheSnakes affiliated with Instagram or Meta? ▼

No. FindTheSnakes is an independent tool and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Instagram or Meta Platforms, Inc. in any way. We simply help you make sense of data you already own.

How do I get my Instagram JSON files? ▼

We have a full step-by-step guide for this! Head over to our Instructions page and we'll walk you through the whole process in about 5 minutes.

The short version: go to Instagram Settings → Accounts Center → Your information and permissions → Export your information → Create export → select JSON format.

How long does it take to get my Instagram data? ▼

The time varies depending on the size of your account. Smaller accounts may receive their download link within minutes, while larger accounts with thousands of followers can take anywhere from a few hours to 24 hours. Once you receive the email with your download link, uploading and analyzing your files is quick.

Privacy & Safety
Do I need to give you my Instagram password? ▼

Absolutely not. FindTheSnakes never asks for your Instagram password and never connects to your Instagram account. We only work with data files that you download yourself directly from Instagram.

Any tool that asks for your Instagram password is putting your account at serious risk. We built FindTheSnakes specifically to avoid that.

Are my Instagram files uploaded to your servers? ▼

No. Your Instagram JSON files are processed entirely in your browser on your own device. They are never sent to our servers at any point.

For paid monthly subscribers, your analysis results (usernames and follow status) are saved to power features like audit history and the unfollower log. But the raw files themselves never leave your device.

Will using FindTheSnakes get my Instagram account banned? ▼

No. Because we never connect to your Instagram account, access Instagram's API, or do anything on Instagram on your behalf, there is zero risk to your account. We simply read files you already own.

Tools that scrape Instagram or use your login credentials carry real risks of account suspension. FindTheSnakes was designed from the ground up to avoid all of that.

What data do you store about me? ▼

For free users — nothing beyond what's needed to serve the page. Your files are processed locally and nothing is stored.

For paid accounts, we store:

  • Your email address and hashed password (for login)
  • Your analysis results (usernames and follow status)
  • Your whitelist (accounts you've hidden)
  • Your notes (private notes on accounts)
  • Your audit history and unfollower log

We never sell your data. See our full Privacy Policy for details.

Features
What is the Whitelist feature? ▼

The whitelist lets you hide specific accounts from your "not following back" results. For example, you might follow a celebrity, a brand, or a news account that will never follow you back — and you don't want to see them cluttering your results every time.

Whitelisted accounts are hidden from your results but never deleted. You can view and manage them anytime from the Whitelist tab. This feature is available on the Pro monthly plan and syncs across all your devices.

What is the Audit History feature? ▼

Every time a Pro subscriber runs an analysis, we save a snapshot of the results. Your audit history lets you look back at past analyses and compare them — so you can see exactly who was not following you back on a specific date and how that has changed over time.

Each audit card shows new unfollowers (people who appeared since your last audit) highlighted in red so you can spot changes instantly.

What is the Unfollower Log? ▼

The unfollower log is a permanent record of every single person who has ever appeared as "not following back" since you started using FindTheSnakes. Unlike the current results view which only shows your latest analysis, the log tracks everyone historically.

Each entry shows when they were first detected, when they were last seen, and whether they are still not following you or have since disappeared (either they refollowed you, you unfollowed them, or their account changed).

What is the Snake List? ▼

The Snake List is your personal hall of shame — a curated list of accounts you want to keep tabs on for your own satisfaction. Maybe they unfollowed you after you supported them for years. Maybe they ghosted you after a collab. Maybe they just rub you the wrong way.

Whatever the reason, the Snake List lets you pin those accounts in one dedicated place so you never forget. It's not about doing anything with the list — it's just yours. A little private record of the people who didn't deserve your follow.

Add anyone by clicking the 🐍 button on their row in your results. View your full list anytime from the Snake List tab. This feature is available on the Pro monthly plan and syncs across all your devices.

What are account notes? ▼

Notes let you add a private reminder to any account in your results. For example you might note "met at conference 2024" or "brand collab - don't unfollow" or "followed me then unfollowed same day".

Notes are completely private, stored securely in your account, and sync across all your devices. They also appear in your CSV exports and printed reports. This feature is available on the Pro monthly plan.

Can I export my results? ▼

Yes! Paid users can export their full results as a CSV file. The export includes usernames, Instagram URLs, follow status, and whitelist status. Pro monthly subscribers also get their notes and audit history summary included in the export.

You can open the CSV in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet app.

Does FindTheSnakes work with large Instagram accounts? ▼

Yes! Instagram splits large follower lists across multiple files (like followers_1.json, followers_2.json, etc.). FindTheSnakes supports uploading multiple files at once for both followers and following — just select them all when uploading and we'll combine them automatically.

Troubleshooting
My results seem wrong or incomplete. What should I check? ▼

A few things to check:

  • Make sure you uploaded the correct files — followers files go in the Followers zone and following goes in the Following zone
  • If you have a large account, make sure you uploaded ALL of your followers files (followers_1.json, followers_2.json, etc.) not just the first one
  • Make sure you selected JSON format when requesting your Instagram export, not HTML
  • Make sure your export includes All time data, not just a recent date range
I paid but my results are still blurred. What do I do? ▼

This can happen if the payment confirmation takes a moment to sync. Try these steps:

  • Wait 30 seconds and refresh the page
  • Make sure you are signed into your account
  • Sign out and sign back in
  • Re-upload your files and run the analysis again

If it's still not working after a few minutes, contact us at [email protected] with your payment confirmation and we'll sort it out immediately.

I see "Could not parse" error when uploading my files. What's wrong? ▼

This usually means one of the following:

  • You uploaded an HTML file instead of a JSON file — make sure you selected JSON format when requesting your Instagram export
  • The file is corrupted or incomplete — try re-downloading your Instagram data
  • You uploaded the wrong file — make sure you're uploading from the followers_and_following folder inside your Instagram export
Someone shows as "not following back" but I know they follow me. Why? ▼

A few possible reasons:

  • Your Instagram data export may be slightly out of date — Instagram takes a snapshot of your data at the time of export, which may not reflect the last few hours of activity
  • The account may have a disabled or restricted profile which shows differently in exports
  • They may follow you from a different account than the one you follow

That's why we label results as "Not following back or profile disabled" rather than stating it as absolute fact. You can always whitelist accounts you know are fine.

FindTheSnakes stopped working after an Instagram update. What happened? ▼

Instagram occasionally changes the format of their data exports. When this happens, our JSON parser may need to be updated to handle the new format. We monitor for these changes and update the tool as quickly as possible.

If you're experiencing this issue, email us at [email protected] and attach one of your JSON files (you can remove any sensitive usernames first) so we can investigate.
