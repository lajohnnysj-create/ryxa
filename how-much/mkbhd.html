The short answer

Marques Brownlee, known online as MKBHD, has built one of the most influential tech-focused media operations on YouTube. Forbes-cited estimates place his net worth at approximately $8.5 million as of 2024, with other industry sources estimating annual earnings between $5 million and $10 million. His income comes from YouTube ad revenue on his main channel and Waveform podcast channel, brand sponsorships, his equity stake in Ridge (where he serves as Executive Board Member and Chief Creative Partner), and merchandise.

What sets Brownlee apart from peer creators isn't the headline number, it's the structure. In 2024, he transitioned from one-off brand deals to an equity-and-board-seat partnership with Ridge, the wallet brand he had previously sponsored. That move signaled a deliberate shift from creator-as-promoter to creator-as-operator, the kind of long-term business stake most YouTubers never get offered.

Where the money comes from

Brownlee's income breakdown reflects a deliberate diversification away from pure ad revenue. While most tech YouTubers depend heavily on AdSense and one-off sponsorship rates, MKBHD's structure leans on long-term partnerships, owned media (the podcast), and equity stakes.

YouTube Ad Revenue
Main channel (20.6M subscribers, 5.3B+ lifetime views) plus secondary channels (MKBHD Shorts, Auto Focus, Waveform). HypeAuditor and SocialCounts estimates place monthly earnings between $29K and $93K, depending on view rates and CPM in the tech vertical.
Ridge (Equity + Board Seat)
In February 2024, Brownlee joined Ridge as Executive Board Member, Equity Investor, and Chief Creative Partner. Designs limited-edition wallet and accessory collections. Specific equity percentage and compensation terms have not been publicly disclosed.
Waveform Podcast
Tech podcast co-hosted with Andrew Manganelli and David Imel, part of the Vox Media Podcast Network. Generates ad and sponsorship revenue separate from the main YouTube channel. 346+ episodes as of April 2026.
Brand Sponsorships
Long-standing relationships with major tech brands. Tech-vertical CPM and sponsorship rates command premium pricing relative to general-interest channels. Specific deal values not publicly disclosed.
Studio Operations
Operates from a dedicated studio in Kearny, New Jersey, with a multi-person production team. Functions more like a small media company than a solo creator setup.
Merchandise & Accessories
Includes the Ridge x MKBHD collection of branded wallets, accessories, and tech-adjacent gear. Specific revenue contributions not publicly disclosed.

The Ridge equity deal

In February 2024, Ridge announced that Brownlee would join the brand as Executive Board Member, Equity Investor, and Chief Creative Partner. According to the company, the deal furthered a relationship that began in 2020 when MKBHD first started sponsorship work with Ridge. The Business Wire release noted that Brownlee would direct brand strategy and design limited-edition wallet and accessory collections.

The deal stands out because it moved beyond the standard creator sponsorship model. Rather than a per-video integration rate or a yearly ambassador contract, Brownlee took an ownership stake plus a board seat. Ridge's announcement called it "the first time" Brownlee had committed to a brand at that level. For context, Ridge reported 40% year-over-year growth at the time of the announcement, with more than four million Ridge Wallet owners in market.
