The short answer

Forbes estimates Alix Earle earned around $8 million as of mid-2025, spanning brand deals, podcast advertising, and business investments. Celebrity Net Worth places her total net worth at $20 million, a steep climb from an estimated $6 million in 2023 and $8 million in 2024. She reportedly commands up to $450,000 for a single sponsored Instagram Story, a figure attributed to Village Marketing founder Vickie Segar via The New York Times.

Earle's rise is recent and fast. She joined TikTok in 2020 as a University of Miami business student, but her breakout came in 2022 when her candid "Get Ready With Me" videos, often discussing acne, mental health, and everyday life, started going viral. By 2025 she had been named to the inaugural TIME100 Creators list and had expanded well beyond social media into modeling, podcasting, network television, and startup equity.

Where the money comes from

Earle's income started as brand deals and has widened into podcasting, modeling, television, and startup equity. The mix below is why her annual figure sits well above her per-post social income alone.

Sponsored Posts & Brand Deals
Reported at up to $450,000 for a single Instagram Story, a figure surfaced by Village Marketing's founder via The New York Times. The "Alix Earle effect" routinely sells products out, and her partners span beauty, fashion, and lifestyle brands including Urban Decay.
Hot Mess Podcast
Launched "Hot Mess with Alix Earle" in 2023 and became one of the first shows on Alexandra Cooper's Unwell Network. The casual, unfiltered format added a steady stream of podcast advertising income.
Modeling
Made history as Sports Illustrated Swimsuit's first-ever digital cover model in 2024, followed by a feature in the 2025 Swimsuit Issue. The SI run marked her shift from influencer to mainstream celebrity.
Investments & Equity
Took an equity stake rather than cash in prebiotic soda brand Poppi, which PepsiCo acquired for roughly $1.95 billion in March 2025, an undisclosed windfall. She also joined a reported $3 million funding round for the canned cocktail brand SipMARGS per Forbes.
Television
Competed on Season 34 of Dancing with the Stars in fall 2025, finishing as runner-up with Valentin Chmerkovskiy. Variety reported the season's three finalists were capped at around $295,000 each.
Super Bowl & Campaigns
Appeared in three 2025 Super Bowl commercials, for Carl's Jr., Hellmann's, and Poppi. A national ad slate of that size signals advertiser demand that reaches well beyond her social feeds.

The GRWM-to-business playbook

Earle's content has always been disarmingly ordinary, getting ready for a night out, talking through her day, which is exactly why brands pay a premium for it. Her audience treats her recommendations like a friend's, and the products she features routinely sell out. That trust, not raw view counts, is the asset.

From that single dynamic she built the now-familiar creator stack: a podcast, a modeling career, network TV, and equity in the products she promotes rather than flat endorsement fees. Poppi is the clearest example of why that structure matters. By taking ownership instead of a one-time check, a brand deal turned into a stake in a company that sold for nearly $2 billion.
