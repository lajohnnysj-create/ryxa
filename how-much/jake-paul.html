The short answer

Forbes ranked Jake Paul #13 on its 2024 Top Creators list with $13.6 million in earnings, measured before his November 2024 fight with Mike Tyson. Fortune reports his pre-Tyson net worth at around $80 million, though TheStreet suggested a $120 million net worth was "not out of the question" once the Tyson fight purse and ongoing boxing income were factored in.

Jake's income mix has shifted dramatically from his early YouTube days. Boxing match purses, his Most Valuable Promotions company, and his Anti Fund venture capital firm now drive the majority of his earnings, with social media revenue playing a supporting role.

Where the money comes from

Jake's revenue comes increasingly from boxing and business ventures rather than direct social media. He has been ranked by Forbes as a top creator four times between 2017 and 2023.

Boxing Match Purses
Jake's primary income source as of 2024. The Tyson fight purse alone was reported to be in the tens of millions. Earlier fights against Tyron Woodley and Anderson Silva also reportedly generated multi-million dollar paydays.
Most Valuable Promotions
Boxing promotion company co-founded in 2021 with career investor Nakisa Bidarian. Promotes fights between other boxers, generating revenue beyond Jake's own appearances.
Anti Fund
Venture capital firm founded by Jake. Invests in startups across consumer brands and creator-economy companies.
YouTube
21+ million subscribers, 7.7+ billion total views. Once his primary income source from 2014 onward. Now smaller share of total earnings.
Brand Sponsorships
Various endorsements tied to both his content and his boxing career. Specific deal values not publicly disclosed.
Acting and Music
Various acting credits and music releases over the years, including his stint on Disney Channel's "Bizaardvark" before his YouTube career took off.

The Mike Tyson fight

Jake's November 2024 boxing match against Mike Tyson at AT&T Stadium was one of the most-streamed fights in history. While the exact purse for both fighters has not been officially disclosed, multiple outlets reported Jake's share was in the tens of millions. The fight was broadcast on Netflix, and the streaming platform's involvement made the event one of the highest-grossing creator-led sporting events to date.

Pre-fight, Jake's net worth was widely reported at around $80 million. Post-fight estimates from outlets including TheStreet suggested his net worth could reasonably reach $120 million when factoring purse plus future boxing pipeline.
