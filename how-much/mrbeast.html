The short answer

Forbes estimates MrBeast personally earned $85 million in 2024 and again in 2025, making him the highest-paid creator on Forbes' Top Creators list for the second year in a row. That figure refers to his personal annual take, not his total business revenue. Beast Industries, the company that houses his YouTube channels, Feastables chocolate brand, and Beast Games show, reportedly generated around $473 million in revenue in 2024, but the company is not yet profitable due to high production costs.

MrBeast himself told Time in early 2024 that his businesses generated between $600 million and $700 million annually. He has consistently said he is not "rich right now" because he reinvests almost everything he earns back into production.

Where the money comes from

MrBeast's revenue spans several distinct businesses. Each one operates differently, and most of his personal earnings come from a mix of YouTube ad revenue, sponsorships, and ownership stakes in his consumer brands.

YouTube Ad Revenue
Estimated $3–5 million per month across his main channel and side channels, based on analyst estimates from Social Blade and Celebrity Net Worth.
Feastables
His chocolate and snack brand. Reported $18 million in sales in a single month in 2024. Projected to break $500 million in annual revenue by 2025 per industry estimates.

Estimated $10–20 million per year across his content. Often integrated into giveaways and stunts.

Virtual restaurant chain reportedly generating $150 million in revenue. Currently in active legal dispute with the operator Virtual Dining Concepts.
Merchandise
Apparel and merchandise sold via the MrBeast store. Multi-million dollar revenue stream though smaller than core businesses.

Net worth: a contested number

MrBeast's net worth is genuinely difficult to pin down because it depends on how you value his ownership stake in Beast Industries.

Forbes places his net worth at roughly $500 million, treating it as a function of accumulated earnings minus the heavy reinvestment he makes back into his content. But Business Insider reports that Beast Industries is valued at around $5 billion, and MrBeast himself confirmed in a November 2024 deposition that he owns "a little over half" of the company. That ownership stake, valued at market, would place his net worth closer to $2.5 billion.

MrBeast has rejected the billionaire framing publicly. In his Time interview, he said: "I'm not naive; maybe one day. But right now, whatever we make, we reinvest." Whether you consider him a billionaire depends on whether you count his Beast Industries equity as personal wealth, which is the standard way valuations work for founder-controlled private companies.
