The short answer

Forbes ranked Ryan Kaji #21 on its 2024 Top Creators list, estimating his annual earnings at approximately $35 million. Ryan was the highest-paid YouTuber in 2018, 2019, and 2020, when his annual earnings peaked around $29.5 million per Forbes. His total net worth is estimated at $100 million as of 2024 per Celebrity Net Worth, though some sources value the broader Ryan's World brand and intellectual property significantly higher.

The Ryan's World empire is run by Ryan's parents through Sunlight Entertainment, the family company that handles his licensing deals. The franchise has expanded far beyond YouTube into licensed merchandise sold at major retailers, a Nickelodeon TV series, video games, a feature film, and over 5,000 branded products.

Where the money comes from

Ryan's income mix is dominated by licensed merchandise and TV/streaming deals rather than direct YouTube ad revenue. The Kaji family has built one of the most diversified kid-creator empires in entertainment.

YouTube Ad Revenue
Ryan's main channel has 36.8M+ subscribers and 57B+ views. Social Blade estimates monthly ad revenue between $61.9K and $991.1K, with annual estimates between $743K and $11.9 million.
Ryan's World Merchandise
Over 5,000 licensed products including toys, apparel, accessories, and home decor sold at major retailers like Walmart. The New York Times reported the family earned at least $25 million from merchandise in 2021.
Nickelodeon TV Series
"Ryan's Mystery Playdate," a 20-episode preschool live-action and animated series, launched on Nick Jr. in 2019 in partnership with PocketWatch.
Amazon Kids+ Series
"Super Spy Ryan," a hybrid live-action/animated series, premiered on Amazon Kids+ in November 2020.
Video Games
Multiple Ryan's World games including "Race with Ryan" (2019), "Ryan's Rescue Squad" (2022), "Tag with Ryan" mobile app, and an official Roblox game launched in 2020.
Feature Film
"Ryan's World The Movie: Titan Universe Adventure" expanded the franchise into theatrical entertainment.

The Sunlight Entertainment angle

Ryan's career is unique among top creators because it operates as a family business through Sunlight Entertainment, the company run by his parents Shion and Loann Kaji. The family signed a deal with kids' media company PocketWatch in 2017, which handles much of the marketing and merchandise. The arrangement has drawn occasional criticism around child labor questions and the family's opaque business practices, though Ryan's parents have publicly stated they reinvest profits into his future and donate many featured toys to charity.

Ryan turned 13 in 2024. The aging-out challenge is a real one: his original preschool audience is now graduating to other content, which may require the franchise to evolve or find new viewers to maintain peak earnings.
