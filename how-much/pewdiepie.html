The short answer

Celebrity Net Worth estimates PewDiePie's total net worth at $45 million as of 2024. At his peak earning year in 2016, Forbes named him the highest-earning YouTuber with an annual income of $15 million, a 20 percent increase from the previous year driven by his YouTube Red series Scare PewDiePie and his bestselling book. Some outlets have suggested his net worth could be significantly higher, with Influencer Marketing Hub estimating $90 million when factoring all investments and merchandise ventures.

PewDiePie has stepped back substantially from his peak career intensity. He has been on YouTube since 2010, held the most-subscribed individual channel for nearly a decade before being surpassed by MrBeast, and continues to upload at a reduced cadence. He moved to Japan with his family in 2022 and his son Bjorn was born in 2023.

Where the money comes from

PewDiePie has built one of the most enduring YouTube careers in the platform's history. While direct earnings have declined from his peak, his career-total income remains among the highest in YouTube history.

YouTube Ad Revenue
111+ million subscribers on his main channel. While he uploads less frequently now, his catalog of older videos continues to generate ongoing revenue.
Book Sales
Published "This Book Loves You," a quote collection released by Penguin Books and Dark Horse Comics. Became a bestseller and contributed to his peak 2016 earnings.
YouTube Red / Original Content
Starred in "Scare PewDiePie," a YouTube Red original series. Helped drive his $15M peak earnings year in 2016.
G FUEL Partnership
Long-running brand partnership with G FUEL energy drinks. Specific deal terms not publicly disclosed but described as his most consistent brand partner.
Merchandise (Represent)
Owns part of Represent, the company that handles his branded merchandise. Generates ongoing royalty income.
Tsuki Clothing (now closed)
Unisex clothing brand launched with his wife Marzia. Reportedly closed by 2025 but added income during its run.

The peak vs. current trajectory

PewDiePie's career peak by Forbes' measure was 2016, when he topped the Highest-Paid YouTuber list at $15 million. He maintained Forbes Top YouTuber rankings through 2017 despite controversies that cost him some partnerships. His current public earnings are not annually reported by Forbes, as he has stepped back from the upload cadence that defined his peak years.

One notable income note: PewDiePie has publicly stated that he relies on external revenue sources beyond YouTube ad revenue, acknowledging that platform ad earnings alone are insufficient and unpredictable. He has also been a notable charitable donor, organizing fundraising drives and encouraging his audience to donate.
