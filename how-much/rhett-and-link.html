The short answer

Rhett McLaughlin and Link Neal ranked #4 on Forbes' 2025 Top Creators list with a combined $36 million in annual earnings, behind only MrBeast ($85M), Dhar Mann ($56M), and Jake Paul ($50M). Their combined net worth is estimated at approximately $36 million per Celebrity Flex. They have appeared on Forbes' highest-paid YouTubers list every year since the publication began tracking the category in 2015.

The duo runs Mythical Entertainment, an 80-person production company based in Burbank, California. Their flagship show Good Mythical Morning has 19.5+ million subscribers and 10.8+ billion total views. Forbes estimated their earnings at $30 million for 2024 alone.

Where the money comes from

Mythical Entertainment's revenue spans every traditional and emerging media stream. According to Forbes, their $36 million annual figure encompasses all Mythical revenue: YouTube ads, brand sponsorships, merchandise, tours, podcasting, publishing, streaming licensing, and venture capital returns.

YouTube (Multiple Channels)
Good Mythical Morning is the flagship with 19.5M+ subscribers. Additional channels include Good Mythical MORE, Mythical Kitchen (3.48M subs), and the Wonderhole scripted comedy series launched 2024.
Mythical Entertainment
80-person production company in Burbank, California. Owns Mythical.com merchandise store, develops original IP across YouTube and streaming.
Ear Biscuits Podcast
Long-running interview and life-discussion podcast. Significant ad revenue stream alongside YouTube content.
Mythical Accelerator
Venture capital fund investing in other creators. Featured creators include Kinigra Deon, Dan and Riya, FunnyMike, Jubilee, and Watcher Entertainment.
Live Tours and Books
Multi-city live performance tours plus book royalties from their published works. Both contribute meaningful revenue.
Brand Partnerships
Long history of partnerships with McDonald's, Amazon, Coca-Cola, Taco Bell, Wendy's, and others. Often integrated into Good Mythical Morning episodes.

The Mythical Entertainment model

Rhett and Link founded Mythical Entertainment in 2017 to consolidate their creator empire under one structured company. Unlike most creator businesses, Mythical operates with the structure of a traditional media company: 80 employees, a CEO (Andrew Yaffe), a Burbank production facility, and venture capital activities. It is one of the most institutional creator companies in existence.

The company has acquired and divested other YouTube brands (notably Smosh, which they later sold). The Mythical Accelerator program now functions as a creator-economy VC arm, taking equity in other creators' businesses.
