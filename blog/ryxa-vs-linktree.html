Ryxa vs. Linktree: Which Link in Bio Is Right for You?

Ryxa 📅 April 2026 ⏱ 6 min read

Linktree is the most well-known link in bio tool in the world. Over 50 million people use it. When someone says "link in bio," most people picture Linktree. That kind of brand recognition is hard to compete with.

But being the biggest doesn't mean it's the best fit for every creator. Linktree was built as a link page. Ryxa was built as a creator workspace that happens to include one. That's a fundamentally different approach, and depending on where you are in your journey, one will make more sense than the other.

This isn't a hit piece. Both platforms do what they do well. Here's an honest look at how they compare so you can decide for yourself.

What Linktree Does Well

Linktree's free plan is solid for the basics. You get unlimited links, a clean page, and simple analytics. If all you need is a list of links in your Instagram bio, Linktree handles that perfectly. It's fast to set up, the interface is intuitive, and the brand recognition means people trust clicking on a Linktree link.

Their paid plans add customization, analytics, email collection, scheduling, and commerce tools. The Starter plan at $8/month gives you better visuals. Pro at $15/month adds email collection, removes branding, and includes Meta Pixel tracking. Premium at $35/month drops the transaction fee to 0%.

If your entire online presence is a list of links and you don't sell anything, Linktree is a great choice. It does that one thing well.

Where Things Get Expensive

The pricing gets complicated once you start selling. Linktree charges transaction fees on lower-tier plans, on top of Stripe's standard processing fees. These can run up to around 12% on the Free plan and 9% on Starter and Pro. Only the Premium plan at $35/month drops the platform fee to 0%.

To put that in perspective: on a $100 digital product sold through Linktree's Pro plan, the combined fees (platform + Stripe processing) come out to roughly $12. Sell 10 of those in a month and fees alone take over $120, on top of the $15 subscription.

Ryxa takes 0% transaction fees on every plan, including free. The only fees you pay are Stripe's standard processing (2.9% + $0.30). When you sell courses or coaching through Ryxa, your revenue after Stripe goes directly to you.

Linktree charges transaction fees of up to 12% on lower-tier plans. Ryxa charges 0% on every plan.

More Than a Link Page

This is where the two platforms diverge the most. Linktree is a link page with extras. Ryxa is a full creator workspace with 15 tools built in.

With Ryxa, your link in bio is one tool inside a dashboard that also includes a course builder, 1:1 coaching bookings, brand deal CRM, media kit, script builder, follower audit, AI design studio, analytics, subscriber management, and more. You don't need to sign up for separate platforms and stitch them together.

Linktree has been expanding beyond links too. They recently added a course builder powered by Kajabi, digital product sales, and commerce features. It's a solid step forward, though their transaction fees still apply on lower-tier plans. If you want coaching bookings, a brand deal CRM, or a media kit, you'd still need external tools.

Branding and Customization

On Linktree, removing the Linktree logo requires a Pro plan at $15/month. Custom backgrounds and advanced themes are locked behind Starter ($8/month) and above.

On Ryxa, removing the Ryxa branding is available on the Pro plan at $10/month. The free plan includes 3 themes, 50 links, and full mobile-first customization. Pro unlocks all themes, removes branding, and adds featured links. Creator Max adds hero links, custom backgrounds, and up to 1,000 links.

Both platforms give you a solid free experience. Ryxa's Pro is $5/month cheaper than Linktree's Pro for essentially the same branding benefit.

Email Collection

Linktree requires a Pro plan ($15/month) to collect emails from your page. On the free plan, there's no email capture.

Ryxa includes email collection on every plan, including free. You can add a subscribe block to your link in bio, collect emails from course and coaching purchases, and manage your subscriber list from the dashboard. You can also export your list as a CSV anytime.

Analytics: Both offer analytics. Linktree's advanced analytics require paid plans. Ryxa's full analytics (page views, revenue, product performance) are included on Creator Max.

Who Should Use Linktree

If you just need a clean link page with unlimited links and you don't plan to sell anything, Linktree's free plan is excellent. It's the industry standard for a reason. The brand recognition alone means your audience trusts clicking on it.

If your strategy is purely about driving traffic to external platforms (YouTube, Spotify, your website) and you don't need built-in commerce, Linktree does that job well.

Who Should Use Ryxa

If you're a creator who wants to build a business, not just share links, Ryxa makes more sense. The link in bio is your storefront, but the dashboard behind it is where you build courses, book coaching sessions, manage brand deals, track analytics, and grow your subscriber list.

If you sell anything (or plan to), the 0% transaction fee saves you real money as your revenue grows. And instead of paying $30-80/month across multiple tools, everything lives in one workspace starting at $10/month.

The Bottom Line

Linktree is a great link page. Ryxa is a creator workspace that includes a great link page. They solve different problems at different price points.

If links are all you need, Linktree works. If you're building a creator business and want everything in one place without transaction fees eating into your revenue, Ryxa is worth a look.
