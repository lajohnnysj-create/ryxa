Ryxa vs. Beacons: Which Creator Platform Is Right for You?

Ryxa 📅 April 2026 ⏱ 6 min read

Ryxa and Beacons are both all-in-one creator platforms. They overlap in a lot of ways. Both offer a link in bio, digital product sales, email tools, analytics, media kits, and AI features. If you've been comparing the two, you're not alone.

This isn't about declaring a winner. Both platforms are actively building for creators. The real question is which approach fits how you work and what you're willing to pay. Here's an honest comparison based on what each platform offers today.

What Beacons Does Well

Beacons has built a strong free plan. You get a customizable link in bio, a digital product store (with a 9% transaction fee), a media kit, basic analytics, and limited email sends. For a creator just getting started, it's a lot of functionality at no cost.

Their AI assistant, Beam, is a standout feature. It helps with content ideas, brand outreach, email drafts, and analytics insights. Beacons has leaned heavily into AI as a core part of the experience, and it shows.

Beacons also offers built-in email marketing with automations, which is something many creator platforms skip entirely. If email is a big part of your strategy, Beacons gives you tools to manage it without a third-party service.

Pricing Comparison

Both platforms follow a tiered model, but the pricing structure is different.

Beacons offers a Free plan, Creator Pro at $10/month, Store Pro at $30/month, and Business Pro at $90/month. Transaction fees on the Free and Creator Pro plans are 9%. To get 0% transaction fees, you need Store Pro at $30/month. Removing Beacons branding requires Business Pro at $90/month.

Ryxa offers a Free plan, Creator Pro at $10/month, and Creator Max at $20/month. Transaction fees are 0% on every plan, including free. Removing Ryxa branding starts at $10/month on Creator Pro.

To remove branding and get 0% fees, Beacons costs $90/month. Ryxa costs $10/month.

That's a significant difference for creators who are selling and want a clean, professional page. On Beacons, you'd need to reach the $90/month Business Pro tier to get both. On Ryxa, both are available at $10/month.

Where They Overlap

Both platforms include link in bio pages, media kits, analytics, digital product sales, and AI-powered tools. Both have free plans that let you start without a credit card. Both are designed specifically for creators rather than general small businesses.

If you're comparing feature lists, they look similar on paper. The differences are in the details: how each feature works, how deep it goes, and what it costs to unlock.

Where They Differ

Email marketing: Beacons includes built-in email marketing with automations and geo-targeted campaigns on paid plans. Ryxa focuses on email collection and subscriber management, with plans to support third-party integrations like ConvertKit and Mailchimp in the future. If native email marketing is important to you today, Beacons has the edge here.

Coaching and appointments: Both platforms offer appointment booking. Beacons includes calendar sync on higher-tier plans. Ryxa includes a dedicated coaching tool with Calendly integration and manual booking options on Creator Max.

Brand deal management: Ryxa includes a full brand deal CRM for managing partnerships, storing contracts, tracking deliverables, and sharing a secure portal with brands. Beacons offers AI-powered brand outreach tools on paid plans, which takes a different approach to the same problem.

Design and content tools: Ryxa includes an AI design studio, script builder, thumbnail analyzer, and contract analyzer as part of the platform. Beacons focuses its AI tools on content suggestions, outreach, and analytics through Beam.

Transaction Fees

This is one of the biggest practical differences. Beacons charges a 9% transaction fee on its Free and Creator Pro plans. That means for every $100 in sales, $9 goes to Beacons before Stripe's processing fees. To eliminate Beacons' platform fee, you need Store Pro at $30/month.

Ryxa charges 0% transaction fees on all plans. The only fees are Stripe's standard processing costs (2.9% + $0.30 per transaction). If you sell courses or coaching through Ryxa, your revenue after Stripe goes directly to you regardless of your plan.

For creators who sell consistently, this adds up. A creator doing $1,000/month in sales on Beacons' free plan loses $90 in platform fees alone. On Ryxa, that $90 stays in their pocket.

Who Should Use Beacons

If built-in email marketing is a priority, Beacons is worth a serious look. Their email tools go deeper than most creator platforms, with automations, geo-targeting, and audience segmentation on paid plans. If you want everything including email marketing under one roof without connecting third-party tools, Beacons delivers that.

Beacons is also a good fit if you value AI-assisted brand outreach and want help finding and pitching to brands directly from the platform.

Who Should Use Ryxa

If you want 0% transaction fees from day one, Ryxa is the clear choice. You don't need to upgrade to a $30/month plan to stop paying platform fees on your sales. That savings matters most for creators who are actively selling courses, coaching, or digital products.

Ryxa is also a strong fit if you want a broader set of creator tools beyond link in bio and sales. The brand deal CRM, design studio, script builder, and contract analyzer are included in the platform and aren't common on other creator platforms.

And if removing branding matters to you, doing it for $10/month instead of $90/month is a straightforward win.

The Bottom Line

Beacons and Ryxa are solving similar problems from different angles. Beacons leans into email marketing and AI-assisted outreach. Ryxa leans into zero-fee monetization and a wider set of creator business tools.

Neither platform is wrong. The right choice depends on what matters most to you: native email marketing, or keeping more of what you earn. Both have free plans. The best way to decide is to try them.
