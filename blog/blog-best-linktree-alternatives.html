Best Linktree Alternatives in 2026 (Honest Comparison)

Ryxa 📅 April 2026 ⏱ 7 min read

Linktree built a $1.3 billion company on a single idea: one link that holds all your other links. It's not complicated. So why are there now twenty different tools competing for the same spot in your Instagram bio?

Because Linktree is not the only option, and for a lot of creators, it's not even the best one. Some alternatives are cheaper. Some look better. Some bundle in tools you'd otherwise pay separately for. The right pick depends on what you actually need.

This is an honest comparison. We'll go through the main contenders, what they're good at, what they're not, and at the end, our pick for most creators in 2026.

"The best link-in-bio tool isn't the one with the most features. It's the one that fits how you actually work."

What to Look For in a Linktree Alternative

Before naming names, here's a quick framework. The link-in-bio space is crowded because the surface-level feature is identical across every tool. The differences live in:

  • Pricing. Free plans range from generous to crippled. Paid plans run from $5 to $25 a month. Watch out for "free" tools that lock essentials behind upgrades.
  • Customization. Some tools give you full theme control. Others limit you to a few presets to keep their branding visible.
  • Branding removal. Most free plans force a "Made with X" banner on your page. Whether you can remove it (and what you pay to do so) varies a lot.
  • Bonus tools. Some link-in-bio tools are starting to bundle other creator features — analytics, media kits, scheduling, even invoicing. These can replace 3-4 other subscriptions.
  • Speed and reliability. Your link-in-bio is in your Instagram bio. If it loads slowly or goes down, you lose followers' trust.

Keep those in mind as we go through the options.

1. Linktree (the original)

Free plan: Unlimited links, basic analytics, mandatory Linktree branding.
Paid: Starts at $5/month for the Starter plan, $9/month for Pro, $24/month for Premium.

Linktree is the default option, and there's a real reason for that. It works, it's reliable, and most creators have used it at some point. The free plan is decent and the analytics on Pro are solid.

The downside is value. Once you start wanting things like custom branding, link scheduling, or the ability to add a video, you're pushed up the pricing tiers fast. The Premium tier at $24/month is hard to justify when you're just hosting a list of links.

Best for: Creators who want the most established option and don't mind the price. People who already have Linktree set up and don't want to migrate.

2. Beacons

Free plan: Generous, includes basic store and email collection.
Paid: $10/month Creator, $25/month Entrepreneur.

Beacons leans hard into the "creator business" angle. Beyond the link-in-bio basics, it lets you sell digital products, collect emails, and even pitch brands through their built-in marketplace.

If you're trying to monetize through your link in bio, Beacons goes further than most. The downside is that the page itself can feel busy. all the extra features show up as recommended sections, and pulling them out takes work. Some creators love this. Some find it cluttered.

Best for: Creators who want to sell products or services directly from their bio link and don't mind the heavier interface.

3. Stan Store

Free plan: None — 14-day trial only.
Paid: $29/month Creator, $99/month Pro.

Stan is built around selling. Coaching calls, courses, digital products. If your link in bio's main job is to convert followers into paying customers, Stan does that well. The checkout flow is smooth and the templates are clean.

That said, it's expensive. There's no free tier, and the entry price is six times what most alternatives charge. If you're not actively selling something, you're paying for features you won't use.

Best for: Established creators with a clear paid offer (course, coaching, product) who want a frictionless storefront.

4. Bento

Free plan: Yes, with light branding.
Paid: $10/month for the Pro tier.

Bento takes the opposite approach to Beacons. instead of cramming features in, it focuses on aesthetics. The pages look closer to a personal portfolio than a list of links. Modular, clean, customizable.

The tradeoff is that it's lighter on creator-business features. There's no built-in store, no email capture out of the box. It's a beautiful page, not a business hub.

Best for: Designers, photographers, and creators who care about how their bio link looks more than what it sells.

5. Carrd

Free plan: Yes, very generous.
Paid: $19/year (yes, year — about $1.58/month).

Carrd isn't really a link-in-bio tool. It's a one-page website builder. But a lot of creators use it as one because it's cheap, flexible, and you can build whatever you want with it.

The catch is that it requires more effort. You're building from scratch with templates rather than dropping in a list of links. If you want quick setup, this isn't it. If you want full control for almost nothing, this is hard to beat.

Best for: DIYers who want a custom page and are willing to spend an afternoon building it. Budget-conscious creators.

How to Actually Decide

Here's the simple framework that should help most creators pick:

  • If you just want a free link-in-bio and don't care about branding: Linktree free or Bento free.
  • If you want the cheapest possible option and don't mind building it yourself: Carrd at $19/year.
  • If you want to sell products or services directly: Stan Store or Beacons.
  • If you want the prettiest page: Bento.
  • If you want link-in-bio plus a media kit, invoicing, and other creator tools in one place: Ryxa.

Notice what's not in there: "the best one." Because there isn't one. The best link-in-bio tool is the one that matches what you're actually going to use.

The Common Mistake Creators Make

The mistake most people make is picking based on what looks coolest rather than what they'll actually use. They sign up for the tool with the most features, then use 10% of them, but pay for 100%.

The right move is to pick based on the smallest set of features that actually matter to you. If you only need a link list, pay for a link list. If you'll genuinely use a media kit and an invoice generator and grid planning, then a bundle saves you money.

One more thing worth saying. Whatever you pick, make sure you can leave. Some tools make it really hard to export your data or migrate elsewhere. The good ones make it easy. If you sign up somewhere and there's no way to get your stuff out, that's a red flag worth noticing now rather than later.

The Bottom Line

The link-in-bio space looks crowded but it really comes down to two questions: what do you actually need, and how much are you willing to pay for it.

For a lot of creators, Linktree is fine and there's no reason to switch. For others, especially anyone juggling multiple subscriptions for related creator tasks, the bundled options are starting to make a lot more sense.
