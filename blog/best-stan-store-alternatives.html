Best Stan Store Alternatives in 2026

Ryxa 📅 April 2026 ⏱ 8 min read

Stan Store has carved out a strong place in the creator monetization world. Mobile-first storefront, courses, bookings, and 0% transaction fees on every plan. The pitch is clean: pay $29 or $99 a month, keep everything you earn. It's helped a lot of creators turn their audiences into income.

The creator platform space has plenty of options, and different tools fit different creators. Knowing what's out there helps you make a more informed pick — whether you're starting fresh or comparing options before committing.

Here are five platforms worth knowing about alongside Stan Store in 2026 — what each does, who it serves, and how to think about choosing between them.

Pick the platform whose pricing model matches your revenue, not the one that markets the loudest.

Alternative #1

Ryxa

Best for: creators who want a complete platform with a real free tier

Ryxa offers what Stan offers — digital products, courses, bookings, link-in-bio storefront — but with a free tier creators can grow on. The Pro and Max plans unlock 0% transaction fees from Ryxa, custom branding, and the AI suite, while the free tier still includes a full bio page, basic media kit, and most everyday creator tools.

The platform extends past selling. Your media kit auto-fills with your Instagram audience data — followers, engagement rate, demographics — labeled "Verified by Instagram" so brands trust the numbers when they view your kit. The dashboard tracks page views, sales, and brand-deal status from one screen. The AI tools help with captions, scripts, and design.

The pricing is structured to grow with you. Start free, upgrade when you're earning enough that 0% fees and Pro features become worth $20-30/month. There's no "you must pay before you've made a dollar" gate.

Best for: creators who want a complete monetization and audience-growth platform, with a free path to start.

Alternative #2

Beacons

Best for: creators who want AI-powered everything

Beacons is the most direct Stan Store competitor in the all-in-one creator platform space. Free tier with a 9% transaction fee, paid plans starting at $10/month (Creator Pro) and going up to $90/month (Business Pro) for 0% fees and white-glove features.

Beacons leans heavily into AI. Brand outreach automation, AI-generated emails, auto-DM features, AI page builder. For creators who want their tools to do more of the work, this is one of the deepest AI-integrated platforms available.

The pricing model is tier-based. The free tier and Creator Pro at $10/month carry a 9% transaction fee on sales, while the Store Pro at $30/month and Business Pro at $90/month drop that to 0%. Choosing the right tier depends on your sales volume and which features you need.

Best for: creators who lean on AI tools and want a free starting point.

Alternative #3

Linktree

Best for: creators who want a simple link page with light commerce

Linktree expanded from pure link-in-bio into a creator commerce platform over the past few years. The free tier handles unlimited links and basic analytics. Paid tiers ($8 to $35/month) add custom branding, email collection, and lower transaction fees on commerce.

For creators who want to sell digital products through their bio without committing to a dedicated storefront platform, Linktree is the most accessible option. The platform has expanded with shop integrations, course features, and bookings, making it a versatile entry point for creators getting started with monetization.

Best for: creators who want a recognizable link page with light commerce on the side.

Alternative #4

Sellfy

Best for: creators selling physical and digital products together

Sellfy is more "indie e-commerce" than "creator platform." The product is a hosted storefront for selling digital products, subscriptions, and physical goods (with print-on-demand support). Creators who sell merch alongside digital downloads find this combination useful.

Pricing starts at $19/month (Starter) and includes digital products, subscriptions, and basic features. Higher tiers ($49/month, $99/month) add upsells, abandoned cart recovery, custom domain, and more product limits. There are no transaction fees beyond standard payment processing.

Sellfy's product centers on the storefront experience rather than a social-first audience hub, making it a strong fit for creators driving traffic directly to their store URL or running a brand around physical and digital products together.

Best for: creators selling both digital and physical products who already have an audience driving traffic to a storefront URL.

Alternative #5

Gumroad

Best for: creators just starting and unwilling to pay monthly

Gumroad has been a fixture in the indie creator world for over a decade. The pitch is simple: no monthly fee, no upfront cost, sell whatever you make. Gumroad takes 10% on direct sales (or up to 30% if a buyer discovers you through their marketplace).

For creators just starting out, the math works. If you sell a $10 product and earn $9 after Gumroad's 10% cut, you're profitable from sale one. The platform skips monthly subscriptions entirely, which lowers the barrier to launching your first product.

Gumroad's model centers on per-sale economics rather than a flat fee. As your sales scale, the percentage cut grows in absolute terms, so creators with high consistent revenue often run the math comparing per-sale fees against flat monthly plans to see which fits their volume best.

Best for: early-stage creators selling their first digital products who don't want a monthly subscription.

How to think about choosing

Each platform fits different creators in different ways. The two most useful questions to ask: how much are you currently selling, and what shape is your business?

For revenue-based decisions, here's a rough framework:

  • Earning under $300/month: Free-tier or per-sale platforms tend to feel friendlier. Ryxa (free tier), Beacons (free tier), and Gumroad (no monthly fee) all give you room to start without a subscription commitment.
  • Earning $300–$1,000/month: The math gets interesting. Flat-fee plans like Stan's $29/month start to compete with percentage-based plans like Beacons' Creator Pro. Run the numbers on your specific revenue.
  • Earning $1,000+/month: Flat-fee plans with 0% commission tend to come out ahead. Stan, Ryxa Pro, and Beacons Store Pro are all worth comparing at this stage.

For tooling-based decisions, ask what shape your business actually is:

  • Mostly digital downloads: Most of these platforms work well. Pick on price and on-ramp.
  • Courses and memberships: Ryxa, Beacons, and Stan all handle this strongly.
  • 1:1 bookings or coaching: Ryxa, Stan, and Beacons all support this.
  • Brand deals as a meaningful revenue source: Ryxa or Beacons. Both have media kit and brand-deal tools built in.
  • Physical products alongside digital: Sellfy is built for that combination.

The best platform is the one that fits both where you are today and where you're heading next.
