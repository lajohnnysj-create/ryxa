Best Stan Store Alternatives in 2026

Ryxa 📅 April 2026 ⏱ 8 min read

Stan Store made a name for itself by simplifying creator monetization. Mobile-first storefront, courses, bookings, and 0% transaction fees. The pitch is clean: pay $29 or $99 a month, keep everything you earn.

But Stan isn't perfect for everyone. The lack of a free plan keeps some creators out. The flat fee doesn't make sense at low revenue. And not every creator's business fits the digital-products-and-bookings shape Stan optimizes for.

Here are five Stan Store alternatives worth considering in 2026 — what each does well, who it's built for, and where it might serve you better.

Pick the platform whose pricing model matches your revenue, not the one that markets the loudest.

Alternative #1

Ryxa

Best for: creators who want a complete platform with a real free tier

Ryxa offers what Stan offers — digital products, courses, bookings, link-in-bio storefront — but with a free tier creators can grow on. The Pro and Max plans unlock 0% transaction fees from Ryxa, custom branding, and the AI suite, while the free tier still includes a full bio page, basic media kit, and most everyday creator tools.

The platform extends past selling. Your media kit auto-fills with your Instagram audience data — followers, engagement rate, demographics — labeled "Verified by Instagram" so brands trust the numbers when they view your kit. The dashboard tracks page views, sales, and brand-deal status from one screen. The AI tools help with captions, scripts, and design.

The pricing is structured to grow with you. Start free, upgrade when you're earning enough that 0% fees and Pro features become worth $20-30/month. There's no "you must pay before you've made a dollar" gate.

Best for: creators who want a complete monetization and audience-growth platform, with a free path to start.

Where it differs from Stan Store: Stan has no free plan. Ryxa lets creators start at zero and scale up.

Alternative #2

Beacons

Best for: creators who want AI-powered everything

Beacons is the most direct Stan Store competitor in the all-in-one creator platform space. Free tier with a 9% transaction fee, paid plans starting at $10/month (Creator Pro) and going up to $90/month (Business Pro) for 0% fees and white-glove features.

Beacons leans heavily into AI. Brand outreach automation, AI-generated emails, auto-DM features, AI page builder. For creators who want their tools to do more of the work, this is one of the deepest AI-integrated platforms available.

The fee structure is the trade-off. To get 0% transaction fees, you need to commit to $30/month or $90/month. The Creator Pro at $10/month still carries the 9% fee. Creators selling at volume should run the math on which tier actually saves money.

Best for: creators who lean on AI tools and want a free starting point, even with the 9% fee.

Where it differs from Stan Store: Beacons has a free tier; Stan doesn't. Stan has 0% fees on every plan; Beacons reserves that for higher tiers.

Alternative #3

Linktree

Best for: creators who want a simple link page with light commerce

Linktree expanded from pure link-in-bio into a creator commerce platform over the past few years. The free tier handles unlimited links and basic analytics. Paid tiers ($8 to $35/month) add custom branding, email collection, and lower transaction fees on commerce.

For creators who want to sell digital products through their bio without committing to a dedicated storefront platform, Linktree is the most accessible option. The product catalog isn't as deep as Stan's, but for selling a few digital downloads or running a simple shop, it works.

Where Linktree falls short of Stan is in commerce depth. There's no native course platform. No built-in bookings the way Stan handles them. Email marketing requires integrations. For creators whose business is mostly digital sales, Stan's purpose-built tools usually feel cleaner.

Best for: creators who want a recognizable link page with light commerce on the side.

Where it differs from Stan Store: Linktree is a link page that gained commerce features; Stan is a commerce platform that gained a link page.

Alternative #4

Sellfy

Best for: creators selling physical and digital products together

Sellfy is more "indie e-commerce" than "creator platform." The product is a hosted storefront for selling digital products, subscriptions, and physical goods (with print-on-demand support). Creators who sell merch alongside digital downloads find this combination useful.

Pricing starts at $19/month (Starter) and includes digital products, subscriptions, and basic features. Higher tiers ($49/month, $99/month) add upsells, abandoned cart recovery, custom domain, and more product limits. There are no transaction fees beyond standard payment processing.

Where Sellfy falls short for creators is the social-first audience workflow. There's no native bio page, no media kit, no Instagram-first design language. It's a storefront, not a creator hub. Creators who need a bio link will pair Sellfy with another tool.

Best for: creators selling both digital and physical products who already have an audience driving traffic to a storefront URL.

Where it differs from Stan Store: Sellfy supports physical products and print-on-demand; Stan doesn't. Stan has the link-in-bio storefront experience; Sellfy doesn't.

Alternative #5

Gumroad

Best for: creators just starting and unwilling to pay monthly

Gumroad has been a fixture in the indie creator world for over a decade. The pitch is simple: no monthly fee, no upfront cost, sell whatever you make. Gumroad takes 10% on direct sales (or up to 30% if a buyer discovers you through their marketplace).

For creators just starting out, the math works. If you sell a $10 product and earn $9 after Gumroad's 10% cut, you're profitable from sale one. Compare to Stan's $29/month — you'd need ~$290/month in sales just to break even on the subscription.

Where Gumroad falls behind is at scale. Once you're earning $1,000+/month, the 10% cut starts feeling expensive. A flat $29/month plan saves $71/month at $1,000 in sales. Gumroad also has limited customization, no native bio page, and no AI tools or brand-deal infrastructure.

Best for: early-stage creators selling their first digital products who don't want a monthly subscription.

Where it differs from Stan Store: Gumroad takes a per-sale cut with no monthly cost; Stan charges flat monthly with no commission. Better for low volume; worse at scale.

How to actually choose

The decision between Stan Store and any of these alternatives usually comes down to two questions: how much do you sell, and what tools do you actually need?

For revenue-based decisions, here's the math at a glance:

  • Earning under $300/month: Stan's $29/month flat fee may not be worth it yet. Look at Ryxa (free tier), Beacons (free tier), or Gumroad (no monthly fee, per-sale cut).
  • Earning $300–$1,000/month: The math gets interesting. Stan's flat fee starts to compete with Beacons' Creator Pro ($10/month + 9% fee). Run the numbers on your specific revenue.
  • Earning $1,000+/month: Stan's 0% fee starts looking strong. So does Beacons' Store Pro ($30/month, 0% fees). Or Ryxa's Pro plan with 0% transaction fees plus the broader feature set.

For tooling-based decisions, ask what shape your business actually is:

  • Mostly digital downloads: Any of these work. Pick on price.
  • Courses and memberships: Ryxa, Beacons, or Stan all handle this well. Linktree and Sellfy are weaker here.
  • 1:1 bookings or coaching: Ryxa, Stan, and Beacons all support this. Pick on UX preference.
  • Brand deals as a meaningful revenue source: Ryxa or Beacons. Both have media kit + brand-deal tools built in. Stan, Linktree, Sellfy, and Gumroad don't.
  • Physical products alongside digital: Sellfy is the only one of these built for that combination.

The platform that's perfect today might be the platform you outgrow next year. Pick the one that grows with you.
