Best Carrd Alternatives in 2026

Ryxa 📅 April 2026 ⏱ 8 min read

Carrd is one of the most popular tools for building simple one-page websites. Cheap, fast, and easy to use: millions of creators, freelancers, and businesses have used it to spin up landing pages, personal sites, and simple bio pages.

But the world of one-page builders and creator platforms has expanded a lot in the past few years. Different tools now serve different creators. Some lean into design, some into commerce, some into all-in-one creator hubs. Knowing what's out there helps you pick the right tool for what you're actually building.

Here are five tools to know about alongside Carrd in 2026: what each one does, who it serves, and how to think about choosing between them.

The right tool depends on what you actually need to do, not just where you'll start.

Alternative #1

Ryxa

Alternative #2

Bio Sites by Squarespace

Best for: minimalists who want polish out of the box

Bio Sites is Squarespace's free link-in-bio offering. It's clean, simple, and slightly more design-forward than most link-in-bio tools. Templates feel curated rather than generic, and the editor is intuitive even if you've never built a page before.

Where it shines is the design side: the typography, the spacing, the default themes all feel professional. You can connect a custom domain, embed videos, and link to your social channels with minimal effort.

Bio Sites is intentionally focused. The product centers on the link-in-bio use case (clean templates, social links, embed support), so creators looking for built-in checkout, bookings, or media kits will typically pair it with another tool or move up to a full Squarespace site.

Best for: people who want a clean, free landing page with curated design templates.

Alternative #3

Linktree

Best for: anyone who just needs the most popular link-in-bio

Linktree is the household name in link-in-bio. Over 50 million users have built one. It's the safe default, every creator's first stop. The product handles the basics (unlimited links, analytics, QR codes) and the design is recognizable enough that visitors instantly know what they're looking at.

Where Linktree shines is recognition. Visitors instantly know what they're looking at, and the brand has become almost shorthand for "creator's link page." The product has expanded over the years to include shop integrations, email collection, and digital product sales.

The platform handles a wide range of creators, from those who just need to share a few links to those running full digital storefronts. The interface stays consistent regardless of how much you're using it for.

Best for: anyone who just wants a working, recognizable link page in five minutes. (A closer look at Linktree's fee structure.)

Alternative #4

Beacons

Best for: creators who want AI-powered tools baked in

Beacons takes the link-in-bio idea and pushes it toward the all-in-one creator platform space. You get a customizable page, digital product storefront, email marketing, media kit builder, and an AI assistant that helps with brand outreach and content generation.

The platform leans heavily into automation. AI brand outreach, AI-generated emails, auto-DM features for Instagram. For creators who want their tools to do more of the work, Beacons has built a real lane here.

Beacons was built by a team focused on the creator economy and has scaled to serve millions of creators globally. The product covers a wide range of monetization formats, with AI integration baked into much of the workflow.

Best for: creators who want AI-powered tools and brand-deal automation built into their platform.

Alternative #5

Notion Sites

Best for: people who already live inside Notion

Notion Sites lets you publish any Notion page as a public website. If you already use Notion to organize your work, your portfolio, or your project notes, you can turn that same workspace into a personal site without learning a new tool.

The big draw is content management. Updating your site is the same as editing a doc. You don't think about HTML, layouts, or builders. You write, and the page updates.

Notion's site styling stays focused on readability over deep visual customization: fonts, layouts, and structure follow Notion's clean editor look. The product is content-first, designed for text-heavy pages rather than highly visual landing pages or commerce.

Best for: writers, knowledge workers, and people whose website is mostly text and links.

How to think about choosing

The honest test: ask yourself what you'll use the page for.

If it's a static landing page for a project or a personal site that won't change much, Carrd or Notion Sites are great fits. Bio Sites if you want a polished, design-forward feel.

If you're a creator and the page is your storefront, where people buy your products, book your time, and find your media kit, a full creator platform like Ryxa or Beacons makes the page one part of a larger system rather than the whole thing. (More on what should actually live on a creator's bio page.)

The most important question is matching the tool to where you're actually heading. Most creators start with a simpler tool and migrate later as their needs grow, so a tool that gives you room to expand can save the migration step entirely.

The best tool is the one that fits both where you are today and where you're heading next.
