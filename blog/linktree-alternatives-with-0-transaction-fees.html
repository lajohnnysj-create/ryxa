Linktree Alternatives with 0% Transaction Fees

If you've searched "Linktree alternatives with 0% transaction fees," you've probably already noticed the awkward thing about Linktree's pricing: their 0% transaction fee tier exists. It's just at the top of the price ladder.

Linktree Premium charges 0% on transactions. The catch is the subscription cost. So the actual question isn't "which platform doesn't take a cut on sales." It's "what does it cost to get to 0%, and what's included at that price?"

This post is the honest comparison, with real numbers.

What 0% Actually Costs on Linktree

Linktree has four tiers. Transaction fees vary by tier. As of mid-2026:

  • Free: $0/month. 12% fee on every digital product sale.
  • Starter: $8/month. 9% transaction fee.
  • Pro: $15/month. 9% transaction fee.
  • Premium: $35/month (~$28 annual). 0% transaction fee.

So yes, you can get to 0% on Linktree. It costs $35/month at the Premium tier, or about $336/year on annual billing.

Pricing changes over time, so verify the current numbers on Linktree's pricing page before making a decision. The structure (free with high fee, paid tiers reducing fee, top tier at 0%) has been consistent for a while.

What Most Creators Don't Realize About the Fee Tiers

The Linktree pricing page presents Premium as "the plan with 0% transaction fees." That's accurate. What's less obvious is the math on when Premium starts paying for itself.

The Premium break-even

If you're on Pro ($15/month), you pay 9% on sales

If you're on Premium ($35/month), you pay 0% on sales

Difference: $20/month subscription, in exchange for skipping 9% on every sale

Premium pays for itself at ~$222/month in sales

Below that, Pro is cheaper. Above that, Premium is cheaper. So if you're consistently selling more than ~$222/month through your link in bio, Premium is the rational choice on Linktree.

The frustrating part for creators just getting started: until you hit that volume, you're paying either 9% (on Pro) or 12% (on Free) on every sale. The 0% option is gated behind hitting a certain revenue level. Below that level, you're absorbing percentage fees on every dollar.

Beyond the Fee: What's Included

The other piece of the comparison is what each platform actually includes at the price you're paying. Two platforms charging "0% on sales" can offer very different sets of features at very different price points.

Things to verify when comparing:

  • Built-in product types. Does the platform support courses, coaching/booking, and digital products natively, or do you need third-party tools?
  • Custom domain. Most platforms gate this behind paid tiers. Whose paid tier is cheapest?
  • Email collection. Owned email lists are some of the most valuable creator infrastructure. Is it included?
  • Removing platform branding. Some plans show "powered by [Platform]" until you upgrade. Affects your brand perception.
  • Live coaching/video tools. Linktree doesn't include video calls at any tier. Most creators selling coaching add Zoom ($13/mo) or similar separately. Does the platform you're evaluating include video natively?
  • Analytics depth. Free plans usually give surface-level stats. Detailed conversion tracking lives in higher tiers.

Across these dimensions, the actual all-in cost of running your business on a given platform is the subscription PLUS what you have to add on top to fill gaps.

The Honest Trade-Off Framework

Two questions to ask before committing to any link-in-bio platform:

1. What's the cheapest tier that gives me 0% fees?

If a platform's only 0%-fee option is at $35/month, that's your real cost when you sell. If another platform's 0%-fee option starts at $10/month, that's a $25/month difference, which is $300/year, which is real money. (We wrote a longer breakdown of how platform fees actually eat into creator income over time.)

2. What's actually included at that 0% tier?

If you sell courses, you need course hosting. If you sell coaching, you need scheduling and ideally video. If you sell digital products, you need delivery. A platform that hits 0% but lacks these forces you to bolt on additional tools (each with their own subscription).

The right comparison isn't headline percentage fees. It's effective monthly cost at YOUR product mix and YOUR sales volume.

Note on payment processor fees: Stripe, PayPal, and similar processors charge ~2.9% + 30¢ per transaction. These are unavoidable on any platform. They go to Stripe, not the link-in-bio platform. The "0% transaction fees" comparison in this post refers to fees charged by the platform itself, on top of payment processor fees.

What Switching Looks Like (If You Decide To)

If you've calculated that another platform is a better deal for your situation, the actual migration is mostly mechanical:

  • Sign up for the new platform and connect your Stripe account. ~15 minutes.
  • Recreate your link list. Most pages have 5-7 links. ~20 minutes.
  • Update your social media bios to point to the new URL. A few minutes per platform.
  • Test the flow by clicking through your own link in bio in incognito.
  • Take down or redirect the old page so visitors don't see a stale version.

Total time: roughly an hour. The friction isn't technical, it's just the inertia of having put the old URL in a bunch of places.

The Bottom Line

"0% transaction fees" by itself isn't a strong enough filter. Several platforms offer it. The right filter is "0% transaction fees at a subscription cost that makes sense for my volume, with the features I actually need included at that tier."

For some creators, Linktree Premium at $35/month is exactly right. For others, a platform at a third of that price with comparable or better features is a better fit. The math is specific to your situation: how much you sell, what you sell, and what tools you need around the link-in-bio page itself.
