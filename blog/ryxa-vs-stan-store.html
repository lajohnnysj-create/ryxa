Ryxa vs. Stan Store: Which Creator Platform Is Right for You?

Johnny L 5 min read

Stan Store has become one of the most popular platforms for creators who sell digital products through social media. It's fast to set up, mobile-first, and built specifically for turning followers into customers. If you've seen creators linking to their "Stan" in their bio, this is what they're using.

Ryxa takes a different approach. It's a full creator workspace with selling as one of many tools, not the only focus. Here's how the two compare so you can decide which fits your needs.

What Stan Store Does Well

Stan Store is built for selling. The storefront is optimized for social media traffic, particularly from Instagram and TikTok, and it's designed so that buying feels seamless. Followers tap through from your bio, see your products, and check out without friction. That mobile-first checkout experience is one of Stan Store's biggest strengths.

The platform includes a course builder, appointment bookings, memberships, subscriptions, and unlimited digital product listings. Their AutoDM feature is a standout, letting you automatically send product links or lead magnets when someone comments a keyword on your Instagram posts. For creators actively selling through social, it's a powerful conversion tool.

Stan Store also takes 0% transaction fees on all plans, similar to Ryxa. You only pay standard payment processing fees through Stripe or PayPal.

What Ryxa Offers Differently

Ryxa includes selling tools (course builder, coaching bookings, and a digital storefront through your link in bio), but the platform extends well beyond commerce. The dashboard also includes a brand deal CRM with a shared portal for brands, a media kit, an AI design studio, a script builder, contract analyzer, thumbnail analyzer, follower audit, analytics, and subscriber management.

The idea is that selling is one part of running a creator business. Managing brand deals, designing content, analyzing contracts, and growing your audience are just as important. Ryxa puts all of that in one workspace instead of requiring separate subscriptions for each.

Stan Store is a storefront built for social selling. Ryxa is a workspace built for running a creator business.

Where They Differ

Getting started: This is a significant difference. Stan Store does not offer a free plan. After a 14-day trial, you're on a paid subscription. Ryxa offers a free plan that includes the link in bio, email collection, and access to most tools. You can use Ryxa without paying anything until you're ready to upgrade.

Pricing model: Stan Store starts at a higher monthly cost than Ryxa's paid plans. Ryxa's Pro plan is $10/month and Creator Max is $20/month. Both platforms take 0% transaction fees, so the comparison comes down to the monthly subscription and what's included at each tier.

AutoDM: Stan Store's AutoDM is a feature Ryxa doesn't currently offer. If Instagram automation is a core part of how you convert followers into customers, Stan Store has a clear advantage here.

Brand management tools: Ryxa includes a brand deal CRM, media kit, and contract analyzer. Stan Store focuses on the selling side and doesn't include tools for managing brand partnerships or analyzing contracts. If brand deals are part of your income, Ryxa covers that natively.

Design and content tools: Ryxa includes an AI design studio, script builder, and thumbnail analyzer. Stan Store doesn't include creative tools, though it does offer email marketing with automations on its higher-tier plan.

Custom domain: Stan Store pages live on the stan.store subdomain. Ryxa bio pages live on ryxa.io. Neither platform currently supports fully custom domains for the bio page, but Ryxa's course and coaching pages are served from your account on ryxa.io.

Who Should Use Stan Store

If you're a creator who sells digital products through Instagram or TikTok and you want a polished, mobile-first checkout experience, Stan Store is purpose-built for that. The AutoDM feature is particularly useful if you rely on Instagram comments and DMs to drive sales.

If selling digital products is your primary business activity and you want a streamlined tool that's focused on maximizing conversions from social traffic, Stan Store delivers that focus well.

Who Should Use Ryxa

If you want to start for free and only pay when you're ready, Ryxa lets you do that. There's no trial period that expires. You can build your link in bio, collect subscribers, and use most tools without spending anything.

If your creator business goes beyond selling, Ryxa covers more ground. Brand deal management, media kits, design tools, analytics, and subscriber management are all included alongside the course builder and coaching bookings. Instead of subscribing to multiple platforms, everything lives in one dashboard.

And if the monthly cost matters, Ryxa's paid plans are significantly lower while still offering 0% transaction fees from Ryxa.

The Bottom Line

Stan Store is a focused, polished selling platform for creators who make money through digital products and social media. Ryxa is a broader workspace for creators who need selling tools alongside brand management, content tools, and analytics.

If you know you're going to sell and you want the best checkout experience from social traffic, Stan Store is a strong choice. If you want a wider set of tools at a lower price point with the flexibility to start free, Ryxa is worth trying.
