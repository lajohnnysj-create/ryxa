FAQ

Common questions about Sign PDF.

Is the signature legally binding?
+
An electronic signature is legally binding in most jurisdictions for most agreements (e-Sign Act in the US, eIDAS in the EU). For contracts requiring qualified electronic signatures or specific compliance certifications, you may need a service like DocuSign. For everyday creator contracts, brand deal addendums, NDAs, etc., this works fine.
Are my PDFs uploaded to your servers?
+
No. Files are processed entirely client-side using JavaScript. Your PDF, your contract, your signature. All stay on your device. We never see them.
Can I send the signed PDF to someone for them to sign?
+
Currently this is a one-person signing tool. After you sign and download the PDF, you can email it to the other party for them to sign separately with their own tool.
What's the file size limit?
+
There's no hard cap, but very large PDFs (50MB+) can slow things down depending on your browser and device. For typical contracts and agreements, performance is instant.
Can I edit the PDF text itself?
+
Sign PDF lets you ADD fields and signatures on top of the PDF. It doesn't edit the underlying text. For full PDF text editing, you'd need a different tool.
