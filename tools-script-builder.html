How it works

From blank page to ready-to-film, in four steps.

Built for creators who write their content before they record it. No more rambling takes or forgotten beats.

Step 1
Tag your platform
Reel, TikTok, YouTube Short, YouTube long-form, podcast, or other. A tag for organization across all your scripts.
Step 2
Build in blocks
Break your script into named blocks: intro, segments, CTA, outro. Drag to reorder freely as you refine.
Step 3
Nail your hook
A dedicated Hook field sits at the top of every script. The first 3 seconds get their own space, separate from the rest.
Step 4
Play it back
Switch to storyboard, teleprompter, or cinematic mode for filming. Read while looking at the camera.

Features

Built for spoken content, not blog posts.

Every feature is designed for the way creators actually write video and audio scripts: modular, iterative, performance-first.

Modular block editor
Build your script in blocks instead of one giant text field. Reorder, rename, delete. Full control over structure.
Dedicated hook field
Your first 3 seconds get a separate field at the top of every script. The most important line never gets buried in the body.
Teleprompter mode
Built-in scrolling teleprompter so you can read while looking at the camera. Adjust speed and font size to match your pace.
Storyboard view
Switch to a card-based layout that lays your script out as separate beats. Useful for planning visuals or pacing before you film.
Cinematic mode
Distraction-free fullscreen playback for filming. Black background, large text, your script and nothing else.
Saved to your account
Your scripts live in your Ryxa account, so they're there when you log back in. Create as many as you want and switch between them anytime.
Edit less, post more

A tight script means a faster edit.

Every "um," every false start, every tangent you cut is time in the editing timeline you'll never get back. The cleaner the take, the less you have to fix afterward.

Planning your beats in blocks before you film means you shoot what you actually need, in order. Less footage to sift through, fewer cuts to make, and content that ships while it's still relevant.

FAQ

Common questions about Script Builder.

Can I use this for any video format?
+
Yes. Script Builder works for short-form (Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts), long-form YouTube, and podcasts. Tag the platform on each script for organization across your library.
What's the difference between this and Notion or Google Docs?
+
Script Builder is built specifically for spoken content. Block-based structure forces clean script architecture (hook → setup → payoff → CTA), and the playback modes (teleprompter, storyboard, cinematic) replace the workflow of copy-pasting your script into a separate prompter app.
Can I export my scripts?
+
Yes. Copy all the text, copy spoken lines only, download as a .txt file, or save as a PDF.
Is there a limit to how many scripts I can save?
+
Up to 100 scripts per account, with up to 50 blocks per script.
